/ THE STUDIO

We started Codegineers because we wanted to do
fewer things, better.

Most agencies and most product teams pull in opposite directions. Agencies are wide and shallow — many clients, junior staff, short engagements. Product teams are deep but slow — months of process before anyone writes a line of production code.

We sit in the gap. Every project is staffed by people who've been doing this for a decade or more. No bench-warmers, no junior-led builds, no “we'll figure out who owns it later.” You get the same team from kickoff to launch, and we tell you exactly who they are before you sign anything.

We work with founders, product teams, and CTOs who care about craft and don't have patience for theatre. If that sounds like you, we'd love to talk.

/ 02 — Process

How we
work.

Predictable, transparent, biased toward shipping. Four phases, no surprises.

/ 01

Discovery

One week. We map the problem, audit what exists, and produce a written technical proposal with scope, timeline, and a fixed price.

/ 02

Design

Two to three weeks. Wireframes become prototypes. Engineers join early so what we draw is what we can actually build.

/ 03

Build

Iterative two-week sprints. You see working software at the end of each one, in a real staging environment.

/ 04

Ship

Production launch with monitoring, on-call coverage for the first 30 days, and a structured handover or ongoing retainer.

/ 03 — Principles

What we
believe.

001

Honest scopes, fixed prices

We don't bill by the hour. We write a proposal, we agree on a price, we ship the thing. If we underestimate, that's our problem to solve — not yours.

002

Ship something every two weeks

Long stretches without working software are a smell. We work in sprints that always end with something running in a real environment.

003

Documentation isn't optional

Every project ends with docs your future team can actually use. We write them as we go, not at the end when we've forgotten how it works.

004

Boring tech beats clever tech

If a problem can be solved by reaching for the well-supported, well-documented tool, we'll reach for that one. Novelty is a tax we won't pass on to you.
